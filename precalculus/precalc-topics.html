1. Graphs

Distance and midpoint formulas

The distance between two points A=(x_1,y_1) and B=(x_2,y_2) in the xy-plane is
d(A,B)=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}
The coordinates of the midpoint M of a line segment with endpoints A=(x_1,y_1) and B=(x_2,y_2) is
M=(\dfrac{x_1 + x_2}{2},\dfrac{y_1 + y_2}{2})

Graph of an equation in two variables

The graph of an equation in two variables x and y is the set of points in the xy-plane the coordinates (x,y) of which satisfy the equation. A point (x,y) is on the graph of an equation when x and y satisfy the equation, and a point (x,y) is not on the graph of an equation when x and y do not satisfy the equation

Intercepts of graphs

Points on the graph of an equation that touch or cross the coordinate axes (or equivalently, a point with coordinates (x,y) that satisfies the equation with either x=0 or y=0) are called the intercepts of a graph.
The x-coordinate of a point on the graph on the x axis is called the x-intercept and the y-coordinate of a point on the graph on the y axis is called the y-intercept. Intercepts may be found using either the equation or the graph of the equation.
x-intercepts and y-intercepts can be found respectively by setting y or x to 0 and solving the equation for x or y.

Symmetries of graphs

  • A graph is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if for every (x,y) on the graph, (x,-y) is also on the graph (this is equivalent to saying that a graph of a equation is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if for all (x,y) satisfying the equation, (x,-y) also satisfies the equation because a point (x,y) satisfying the equation is equivalent to (x,y) being on the equation's graph).
  • A graph is symmetric with respect to the y-axis if for every (x,y) on the graph, (-x,y) is also on the graph (can also be viewed from the perspective of sets (x,y) satisfying the graph's equation).
  • A graph is symmetric with respect to the origin if for every (x,y) on the graph, (-x,-y) is also on the graph (can also be viewed from the perspective of sets (x,y) satisfying the graph's equation).
Equations can be tested for symmetries directly without their graphs.
  • The graph of an equation is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if an equivalent equation results from replacing all y with -y in the equation.
  • The graph of an equation is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if an equivalent equation results from replacing all x with -x in the equation.
  • The graph of an equation is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if an equivalent equation results from replacing all x with -x and y with -y in the equation.
Equations may sometimes be more easily graphed when properties of equations such as intercepts and symmetries are known.

Lines

The graph of a linear equation in two unknowns in a xy-plane is a line, and any line in a xy-plane can be represented by a linear equation in two unknowns.

Slope

The slope of a nonvertical line in a xy-plane can be thought of as a measure of the line's "steepness"
Let (x_1,y_1) and (x_2,y_2) be two distinct points on a nonvertical line, the slope m of the line is defined as
m=\dfrac{x_2-x_1}{y_2-y_1}\quad x_1 â‰  x_2
The slope of a nonvertical line is the same regardless of the two points chosen.
The slope of a vertical line is undefined.

Equations of a line

The equation of a vertical line is
x=a
where a is the x-intercept of the line.
The equation of a horizontal line is
y=b
where b is the y-intercept of the line.
The point-slope form of the equation of a line is a form of the equation for a nonvertical line. The equation of a nonvertical line with slope m that contains point (x_1,y_1) is
y-y_1=m(x-x_1)
The slope-intercept from of the equation of a line is a form of the equation for a nonvertical line. The equation of a nonvertical line with slope m and y-intercept b is
y=mx+b
The equation of the general form or standard form of a line is a form of the equation for any line in the xy-plane. It is written as
Ax+By+C=0
or sometimes
Ax+By=C
where A, B, and C are real numbers and A and B are not both 0.

Parallel and perpendicular lines

Two nonvertical lines are parallel if and only if their slopes are equal and they have different y-intercepts. (The if and only if means that two separate statements, one the converse of the other are made. In this case it means 1. if two nonvertical lines have equal slopes and different y-intercepts, they are parallel; 2. if two nonvertical lines are parallel, they have equal slopes and different y-intercepts.)
Two nonvertical lines are perpendicular if and only if the product of their slopes equals -1.

Circles

In the xy-plane, a circle is the set of all points a fixed distance r from a fixed center (h,k). The fixed distance r is the radius of the circle, and the fixed point (h,k) is the center of the circle.
The standard form of an equation of a circle with radius r and center (h,k) is
(x-h)^2+(y-k)^2=r^2
When the center is the origin (0,0), the equation can be written as
x^2+y^2=r^2
The circle with radius r=1 and center at origin (0,0) is called the unit circle, with equation
x^2+y^2=1
When the graph of an equation is a circle, the general form of an equation of a circle is an equation of the form
x^2+y^2+ax+by+c=0
The equation x^2+y^2+ax+by+c=0 with arbitrary real a, b, and c represents a circle only for some a, b, and c. In other cases, it may represent one point or no points.
An equation of a circle can be converted between the standard form and general form.

2. Functions and their graphs

Functions

A function is a relation that associates two sets (usually the set of real numbers or a subset of the set of real numbers, but also may be the set of complex numbers or other sets) X and Y such that each element of X is associated with exactly one element of Y
The set X is called the domain of the function. For each element x in X, the corresponding element y in Y is called the value of the function at x, or the image of x. The set of all images of the elements of the domain is called the range of the function. The range is always a subset of Y, but is not necessarily equal to Y. A function may be a "one-to-one" or "many-to-one" relation, but a "one-to-many" relationship is not a function. The value of a function for a given input is determined by the input.
A function may be represented as a letter, such as f, and the notation f(x)represents the value of f for input x. f(x) is also sometimes to represent the function itself.

Representations of functions

A function may be represented as a diagram of the relation between two sets X and Y that indicate the association between elements in X and Y.
A function may be represented by an equation. For an equation to represent a function, every value of the independent variable in the domain of the function must have exactly one corresponding value of the dependent variable.
For example, the equation x + y = 3 can represent a function with independent variable x and dependent variable y. The function can be rewritten as y = x - 3 to show that every x in the domain (in this case the set of real numbers) corresponds to exactly one y.
As another example, the equation y^2 = x does not represent a function with independent variable x (with the domain being the set of real numbers) and dependent variable y. Solving for y, we see that y = \pm \sqrt{x}, meaning that except for x = 0, every x corresponds to two distinct y's.
A function may also be specified by its graph. A graph is a set of points. A set of points (x, y), either represented by a graph or otherwise, represents a function with independent variable x and dependent variable y if and only if there is at most one point with any given x.
