1. Graphs

Distance and Midpoint formulas

The distance between two points A=(x_1,y_1) and B=(x_2,y_2) can be found by
d(A,B)=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}
The coordinates of the midpoint M of a line segment with endpoints A=(x_1,y_1) and B=(x_2,y_2) can be found by
M=(\dfrac{x_1 + x_2}{2},\dfrac{y_1 + y_2}{2})

Graph of an equation in two variables

The graph of an equation in two variable x and y is the set of points in the xy-plane the coordinates (x,y) of which satisfy the equation. A point (x,y) is on the graph of an equation when x and y satisfy the equation, and a point (x,y) is not on the graph of an equation when x and y do not satisfy the equation

Intercepts of graphs

Points of a graph that touch or cross the coordinate axes (or equivalently, a point with coordinates (x,y) that satisfies the equation with either x=0 or y=0) are known as the intercepts of a graph.
The x-coordinate of a point on the graph on the x axis is known as the x-intercept and the y-coordinate of a point on the graph on the y axis is known as the y-intercept. Intercepts may be found using either the equation or the graph of the equation.
x-intercepts and y-intercepts can be found respectively by setting y or x to 0 and solving the equation for x or y.

Symmetries of graphs

  • A graph is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if for every (x,y) on the graph, (x,-y) is also on the graph (this is equivalent to saying that a graph of a equation is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if for all (x,y) satisfying the equation, (x,-y) also satisfies the equation because a point (x,y) satisfying the equation is equivalent to (x,y) being on the equation's graph).
  • A graph is symmetric with respect to the y-axis if for every (x,y) on the graph, (-x,y) is also on the graph (can also be viewed from the perspective of sets (x,y) satisfying the graph's equation).
  • A graph is symmetric with respect to the origin if for every (x,y) on the graph, (-x,-y) is also on the graph (can also be viewed from the perspective of sets (x,y) satisfying the graph's equation).
Equations can be tested for symmetries directly without their graphs.
  • The graph of an equation is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if an equivalent equation results from replacing all y with -y in the equation.
  • The graph of an equation is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if an equivalent equation results from replacing all x with -x in the equation.
  • The graph of an equation is symmetric with respect to the x-axis if an equivalent equation results from replacing all x with -x and y with -y in the equation.
Equations may sometimes be more easily graphed when properties of equations such as intercepts and symmetries are known.

Lines

The graph of a linear equation in two unknowns in a xy-plane is a line, and any line in a xy-plane can be represented by a linear equation in two unknowns.

Slope

The slope of a nonvertical line in a xy-plane can be thought of as a measure of the line's "steepness"
Let (x_1,y_1) and (x_2,y_2) be two distinct points on a nonvertical line, the slope m of the line is defined as
m=\dfrac{x_2-x_1}{y_2-y_1}\quad x_1 â‰  x_2
The slope of a nonvertical line is the same regardless of the two points chosen.
The slope of a vertical line is undefined.

Equations of a line

The equation of a vertical line is
x=a
where a is the x-intercept of the line.
The equation of a horizontal line is
y=b
where b is the y-intercept of the line.
The point-slope form of the equation of a line is a form of the equation for a nonvertical line. The equation of a nonvertical line with slope m that contains point (x_1,y_1) is
y-y_1=m(x-x_1)
The slope-intercept from of the equation of a line is a form of the equation for a nonvertical line. The equation of a nonvertical line with slope m and y-intercept b is
y=mx+b
The equation of the general form or standard form of a line is a form of the equation for any line in the xy-plane. It is written as
Ax+By+C=0
or sometimes
Ax+By=C
where A, B, and C are real numbers and A and B are not both 0.

Parallel and perpendicular lines

Two nonvertical lines are parallel if and only if their slopes are equal and they have different y-intercepts. (The if and only if means that two separate statements, one the converse of the toher are made. In this case it means 1. if two nonvertical lines have equal slopes and different y-intercepts, they are parallel; 2. if two nonvertical lines are parallel, they have equal slopes and different y-intercepts.)
Two nonvertical lines are perpendicular if and only if the product of their slopes equals -1.

Circles

In the xy-plane, a circle is the set of all points a fixed distance r from a fixed center (h,k). The fixed distance r is the radius of the circle, and the fixed point (h,k) is the center of the circle.
The standard form of an equation of a circle with radius r and center (h,k) is
(x-h)^2+(y-k)^2=r^2
When the center is the origin (0,0), the equation can be written as
x^2+y^2=r^2
The circle with radius r=1 and center at origin (0,0) is known as the unit circle, with equation
x^2+y^2=1
When the graph of an equation is a circle, the general form of an equation of a circle is an equation of the form
x^2+y^2+ax+by+c=0
The equation x^2+y^2+ax+by+c=0 with arbitrary real a, b, and c represents a circle only for some a, b, and c. In other cases, it may represent one point or no points.
An equation of a circle can be converted between the standard form and general form.
